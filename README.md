# Hi, I'm Ayush

I study econ and information science at the University of Maryland. I build
tools that test trading and forecasting ideas the honest way: paper first,
rules written before the run, and a public scoreboard either way. Some ideas
win. The ones that lose stay published, because a dead strategy with receipts
is worth more than a live one with promises.

### Flagships

| Project | What it is |
|---|---|
| [manyworldz](https://github.com/akadigari/manyworldz) | A crowd of AI forecasters. Each agent researches and votes (or simulates the outcome K different ways), the votes fold into one probability, and every pick is logged and graded against real market prices. Paper only. |
| [meridian](https://github.com/akadigari/meridian) | A free macro dashboard that scores ~197 countries on the reform-turnaround playbook, on live IMF and World Bank data that refreshes itself weekly. [Live site](https://meridian-zeta-neon.vercel.app) |
| [zenkei](https://github.com/akadigari/zenkei) | A free personal finance app that runs 100% in your browser. No login, no server, your data never leaves your device. [Live site](https://akadigari.github.io/zenkei/) |

### The honest lab

Backtests where the rules were locked before the run and the verdict got
published either way:

- [trendlab](https://github.com/akadigari/trendlab): ETF trend following. Verdict: the premium is real, my timing edge is not.
- [mechlab](https://github.com/akadigari/mechlab): a popular day-trading "mech model," tested with six pre-registered gates. Verdict: not an edge (failed 5 of 6).

### Also

- [clause](https://github.com/akadigari/clause): a document explainer that cites the exact clause it read, and says "I don't know" instead of guessing.
- [cite](https://github.com/akadigari/cite): a citation formatter with 43 professional styles. Paste a DOI, arXiv ID, ISBN, URL, or PDF.
- [mooncross](https://github.com/akadigari/mooncross): a motocross browser game where every track is a real crypto price chart.

### How I work

Plain English, small commits, tests before claims, no profit promises. If a
project's honest answer is "no edge," that is the published answer.
